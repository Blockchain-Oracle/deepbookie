# Pricing: SVI → N(d₂)

When you ask *"what are the odds BTC is above $70k on Friday?"*, DeepBookie reads
the live market and answers with a real probability.

<OddsCurve />

## In plain terms

1. The market has a **volatility curve** — how much traders expect the price to
   move. DeepBookie fits it with a model called **SVI** (Stochastic Volatility
   Inspired).
2. It converts that into a probability with **N(d₂)** — a standard Black-Scholes
   formula that turns volatility into a 0–100% chance.
3. Do that for every strike and you get the odds curve above.

That probability *is* the fair price of your bet. Higher odds cost more to buy;
longer-shot odds pay more if they hit.

<Callout type="tip">
  This is the same model behind the odds-curve widget in chat. Ask "what are the
  odds BTC closes above $70k?" to see it live.
</Callout>

## For the curious — the math

In `@deepbookie/predict-client`'s `math.ts`:

- `upProbability(svi, forward, strike)` computes a normalized distance `d₂` and
  runs it through `normalCdf()` → a number from `0` to `1`.
- `downProbability = 1 − upProbability`.
- `buildCurve()` samples ~25 strikes around the forward price to draw the smile.

All inputs are scaled ×1e9 (see [Scaling & units](/concepts/scaling)); the
probabilities come back as `0`–`1`.
